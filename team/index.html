Our Team

SU LABS is led by a team of professionals with deep experience in startups and entrepreneurship – always with a strong eye toward global impact. We are fiercly passionate about helping the next generation of entrepreneurs successfully tackling the world’s most intractable problems.

Pascal Finette

Vice President, SU LABS

Pascal heads up the startup initiatives at Singularity University where he grows startups tackling the world’s most intractable problems leveraging exponential technologies.

He loves technology and believes that the Internet is deeply impacting mankind. He got started on the Net before there was a web browser, founded a couple of technology startups, led eBay’s Platform Solutions Group in Europe, launched a consulting firm helping entrepreneurs with their strategy & operations, invested into early-stage tech startups, led Mozilla’s Innovation Lab, created Mozilla’s accelerator program WebFWD, headed up Mozilla’s Office of the Chair and invested into social impact organizations around the globe at Google.org. He also founded the non-profit organizations Mentor for Good, POWERUP and The Coaching Fellowship; the GyShiDo (Get Your S%#& Done) movement and publishes the opinionated The Heretic newsletter.

Monique Giggy

Director, SU Accelerator

Monique Giggy runs the Singularity University Accelerator where she works closely with the team to create a program that truly accelerates companies through curated networking, experience based coaching and a solid focus on metrics.

She is a seasoned value creator with proven success building/exiting companies, driving turnarounds, and leading high performing global teams. Prior to SU she conceived and helped build the world’s most popular and successful mobile golf application- Swing by Swing Golf which she sold in 2014. After the sell of her company she participated in the 2014 Global Solutions Program where she fell in love with SU and discovered new passions in biotech, AI and future forecasting.

Monique has served as an advisor and entrepreneurship coach, Venture Partner, CMO, and has started and invested in several companies.

Archie Japaridze

Program Manager, Mentorship Program

Archie is the Community and Partnerships manager for the SU LABS startup companies. In this capacity, he helps to engage with and expand the SU support network. He is also in charge of the Entrepreneur-in-Residence program.

Prior to SU, he worked with the Unreasonable Institute, a social-enterprise accelerator in Boulder, CO. At Unreasonable, Archie helped to support entrepreneurs in the educational, energy and healthcare industries. In addition to UI, he worked for MANA Nutrition to help alleviate Severe Acute Malnutrition in Sub-Saharan Africa through the use of quantified-self devices.

Originally from the Republic of Georgia, Archie went to the University of Georgia(USA), and speaks fluent Georgian.

Mikhail Holst

Program Manager, SU Startup Network

Bay Area native. Background in computer science but quickly tired of web design and apps. Joined SU LABS in 2012 to develop SynBio Startup Launchpad and has since built out Startup Program. Passionate about using technology for social impact and mitigating future global risks. Occasionally writes for an irreverent magazine in Los Angeles.

Robert Suarez

Managing Director Design & Innovation, SU LABS

Robert is the Managing Director Design & Innovation for Singularity University Labs. SU LABS brings together entrepreneurs, corporations and field impact partners to enable the creation of sustainable solutions to difficult challenges — education, energy, environment, food, global health, poverty, security, space and water — that face humanity.

Before joining SU, he was on the founding team of Level Money, a mission-driven design-led financial services organization in San Francisco. Level’s mission is to empower America’s young adults to take control of their financial futures, both individually and collectively, by creating fair and trustworthy personal financial services and by educating and advocating for financial sustainability.

Throughout his 15-year career in consumer experience design Robert has created product and service experiences that change the way people live, work, and play. He was a Senior Portfolio Director at IDEO, where his design direction focused on building new offerings in mobile experiences, financial services, cross-platform gaming and emerging technologies. Robert strives for solutions that integrate social, environmental, and educational considerations while proving that purpose and profit can combine for the greatest impact.

Jody Medich

Director of Design, SU LABS

Jody is the Director of Design for SU Labs. She helps start-ups to productize their ideas, and create real solutions to the world's toughest problems. She also speaks about Augmented and Virtual Reality for SU.

In her 20-year design career, Jody has created just about everything from holograms to physical products and R&D for over 300 companies. She’s spent the last 5 years on AR/VR, most notably as Principal Experience Designer on the HoloLens Project at Microsoft and Principal UX at LEAP Motion. Previously, she co-founded and directed Kicker Studio, a design consultancy specializing in Natural User Interface and R&D for companies including Intel, Samsung, Microsoft, and DARPA. Jody is also a practicing artist with an MFA in Painting and Design & Technology from the San Francisco Art Institute. She’s a collaborator with the art crew FiveTonCrane, and builds robots and rockets in her spare time.

Barbara Krause

Senior Corporate Counsel, Singularity University

As senior corporate counsel, Barb’s role is to support the legal and business needs of the entire organization. Practically, in any one day, this could range from strategizing about legal and business issues involved in a particular product offering, to interfacing with SU LABS companies or Graduate Studies Program companies on a particular issue, to working through an operations opportunity for SU.

Before joining Singularity, Barb represented leading entrepreneurs, investors and lending institutions for nearly 15 years through AMLAW 100 law firms, as in-house counsel and as a solo practitioner. Initially as a corporate finance lawyer with top-tier global law firms (Bryan Cave, DLA Piper and Bingham McCutchen) , Barb focused her practice on debt and equity finance, mergers and acquisitions, and general corporate concerns. Barb shifted her focus to represent emerging growth companies at the forefront of community led change through her own practice, specializing in entity formation, best board practices, compliance, debt and equity finance, employment and equity incentives, intellectual property protection and licensing, commercial contracts, M&A, and general business counseling. Barb has worked in-house on a project basis and as outside general counsel to leading Bay Area public and private companies throughout her career.
